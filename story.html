Our Story

A burger built on Dad's recipes.

THE HUB started in 2023 with a flat-top, a family spice blend, and a stretch of Hillside Ave that didn't have a real halal smashburger yet. Three years and one Maple Smash later, the line still wraps around the block.

What we won't budge on

Four non-negotiables.

There are a thousand corners we could cut. We don't. These four things stay the same — whether it's a slow Tuesday or a holiday market rush at Union Square.

01 · ZABIHA HALAL

Halal isn't a marketing word.

Every cut of beef and chicken is sourced from Zabiha-certified suppliers and audited annually. Our HUB sauce, marinades, and even our croissants are vetted ingredient-by-ingredient. If we wouldn't serve it to our own family, it doesn't go on the menu.

✓
02 · FARM TO TABLE

Real ingredients only.

Produce is sourced weekly from regional farms — onions from Long Island, tomatoes from Jersey, eggs from a pasture operation in upstate NY. Our maple syrup is single-origin from Vermont. The croissants are baked by a small Astoria bakery, every morning.

🌾
03 · MADE TO ORDER

No patties on standby.

Patties are smashed when you order. Buns are buttered and toasted when you order. The fries are cut twice a day. It means you wait six to nine minutes for a burger. It also means the burger is worth it.

🔥
04 · NEIGHBORHOOD FIRST

Built for the block.

Same prices Year One as Year Three. Free water always. Smiles always free (it's on the menu). Open until 2am because the night-shift crowd deserves a real meal too. And we'll never franchise — one HUB, one block.

♥
The Maple Smash Origin

How a breakfast accident went viral.

It was supposed to be a chicken-and-waffle. Farrukh had spilled maple syrup on a smashburger getting plated for a customer. He kept it. Set up a second order. Asked her to compare. She kept the spilled one.

A week later we added it to the menu as a joke — "The Maple Smash" — for $11. The first photo on Instagram got 12,000 saves. Within a month it was outselling the original.

Three years later it's our signature, and the reason most people drive in from out of borough. We've changed nothing about it. Same patty. Same egg. Same single-origin Vermont maple syrup. Same accident.

"I wasn't trying to invent anything. I just had a customer with a sweet tooth and a clumsy elbow. The block did the rest."

— Farrukh, Head of Kitchen
Receipts

A short history.

Three years, two locations, one block. We'd say a lot has changed but honestly the spice blend hasn't.

'22

The food truck

Started as a small white truck parked on Hillside on weekends. Three menu items: smashburger, chopped cheese, fries. Cash only. Closed at midnight.

'23

The brick-and-mortar

Took over the storefront at 235-20 Hillside Ave. Kept the truck for a year as backup. Hours pushed to 2am. The OG breakfast sandwich joined the menu.

'24

The Maple Smash goes viral

A TikTok from a regular got 4.2M views. The line started wrapping the block. We added a third grill and a permanent prep team.

'25

Union Square Holiday Market

First seasonal pop-up at the Union Square Holiday Market. Sold out every weekend. The Viral Lacha Paratha Burger debuted there.

'26

Today

Two locations, a full breakfast menu, a partnership with The Happy Bakers for stuffed cookies, and a Ramadan giving program that funds meals in Palestine, Sudan, and other Muslim countries.

Ramadan Mubarak

A portion of every Ramadan order feeds families abroad.

During Ramadan, a portion of all our proceeds is donated to verified relief efforts in Palestine, Sudan, and other Muslim communities — covering iftar meals, water, and emergency aid. We post the receipts every Eid.

2026 partners: Islamic Relief USA · Penny Appeal USA · Local masjid initiatives across Queens.
